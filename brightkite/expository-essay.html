Writing Guide: Writing an Expository Essay

The expository essay

The expository essay is a genre of essay that requires the student to investigate an idea, assess signs, expound on the thought, and set forth an argument concerning that thought in a concise and clear way. This can be achieved through contrast and comparison, for example; globalization cause and effect essay.

Here are a few guidelines for writing an expository essay:

A clear, concise, and defined thesis statement occurring in the first paragraph of the essay

It is vital this thesis statement be suitably narrowed to follow the guidelines set forth in the homework. If the student doesn't master this part of the essay, it will not be rather easy to compose a powerful or convincing essay.

Logical and clear transitions between body, the opening, and decision

Transitions are the mortar that holds the basis of the essay. Without logical progression of idea, the reader is not able to follow the essay’s argument, and the construction will fail.

Body paragraphs that contain evidential support

Each paragraph should be restricted to the exposition of one general thought. This will allow for direction and clarity through the entire essay. Moreover, such conciseness creates an ease of readability for one’s audience. It is necessary to notice that each paragraph in the body of the essay must have some reasonable link to the thesis statement in the opening paragraph.

Evidential support (whether factual, logical, statistical, or anecdotal)

Often times, pupils must compose expository essays with little or no preparation; so, such essays don't usually allow for a great deal of factual or statistical evidence.

A little imagination!

Though artfulness and imagination aren't consistently related to essay writing, it's an art form yet. Do your best not to get stuck at the expense of writing something fascinating on the formulaic nature of expository writing. Recall, though you may not be crafting the next great novel, you're trying to leave an enduring impression on the folks assessing your essay.

A conclusion that will not merely restate the thesis, but readdresses it in light of the evidence supplied

It's at this stage of the essay that pupils will necessarily start to fight. This is the part of the essay that may make the immediate impression on the mind of the reader. Thus, it must be valid and powerful. Don't add any new info into the decision; instead, synthesize and come to a conclusion concerning the information.

An entire argument

Maybe it's helpful to think of an essay when it comes to discussion or a dialogue with a classmate. There would be a start, middle, and ending to the dialogue, if I were to discuss the cause of the Great Depression and its present effect on those who lived through the tumultuous time. The truth is, if I were to stop the exposition at the center of my second point, questions would arise concerning the present effects on those who lived through the Depression. Thus, the expository essay must be logically so, and complete, leaving no doubt concerning argument or its purpose.
The five-paragraph Essay

A standard way of writing an expository essay is the five-paragraph strategy. This is, nevertheless, by no means the only formula for writing such essays. That's because it's in case it seems clear-cut; in fact, the system consists of: an opening paragraph, three evidentiary body paragraphs, a conclusion.
