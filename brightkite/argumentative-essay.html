The Argumentative Essay

The argumentative essay is a genre of writing which requires the student to investigate a subject; create, gather, and evaluate signs; and confirm a position on this issue in a concise manner.

No matter the quantity or kind of research concerned, argumentative essays follow sensible reasoning and must confirm a clear thesis.

A clear, concise, and defined thesis statement occurring in the first paragraph of the essay

Transitions are the mortar that holds the basis of the essay. Without logical progression of an idea, the reader is not able to follow the essay’s argument, and the construction will fail. Transitions introduce the notion which will follow in the next section and should wrap up the thought from the preceding section.

Body paragraphs that contain evidential support

Each paragraph should be restricted to the discussion of one general thought. This will allow for guidance and clarity through the entire essay. Furthermore, such conciseness creates an ease of readability for one’s audience. It is necessary to notice that each paragraph in the body of the essay must have some reasonable link to the thesis statement in the opening paragraph. The thesis statement will be directly supported by some paragraphs with evidence gathered during research. In addition it's crucial that you clarify how and why the evidence supports the thesis (warrant).

Nevertheless, argumentative essays describe and should additionally contemplate differing points of view regarding this issue.
